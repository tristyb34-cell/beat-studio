1. Overview

Sqweeky Clean Productions is a grid-based music production tool that runs entirely in your browser. No downloads, no plugins, no accounts. Just open it and start making music.

The concept is simple: you have a grid of rows and beats. You drag sounds from the library and drop them onto the grid. The playhead moves left to right and plays everything it hits. Layer sounds across rows, tweak them with the sound editor, and build full tracks.

All sounds are generated using Web Audio synthesis, so everything works offline. You can also import your own audio files.

2. The Interface

Top Bar

The header contains the transport controls (play, stop, loop), BPM setting, position display, snap setting, and action buttons (templates, export, save, load, versions, import sounds, tutorial).

Sound Browser (Left Panel)

The left sidebar shows all available sounds, organized by category. Click the category name to expand/collapse it. Use the search bar to filter sounds by name.

The Grid (Center)

The main workspace. Rows run vertically (numbered 1, 2, 3...) and beats run horizontally. Hard lines mark bar boundaries (every 4 beats), soft lines mark individual beats.

Each row has controls on the left: row number, mute (M) button, solo (S) button, and delete (X) button. These are sticky, so they stay visible as you scroll horizontally.

Ruler (Top of Grid)

Shows bar numbers along the top. Scrolls in sync with the grid. You can also set loop regions by dragging on the ruler.

Sound Editor (Bottom Panel)

Appears when you double-click a placed sound. Contains knobs and sliders for pitch, volume, pan, filter, distortion, and more.

Previewing Sounds

Click the small play button next to any sound in the browser to hear it. This lets you audition sounds before placing them on the grid.

Importing Your Own Sounds

Click the "+ Sounds" button in the top bar to import audio files from your computer. Supported formats: WAV, MP3, OGG, FLAC. Imported sounds appear in a "User" collection in the browser.

4. Placing Sounds

Drag and Drop

Click and drag a sound from the library panel on the left, then drop it onto any cell in the grid. The sound will snap to the nearest beat position based on your snap setting.

Sound Sizing

  • Short sounds (kicks, snares, hats) fill 1 beat by default
  • Longer sounds (pads, bass, strings) fill their natural duration
  • You can resize any placed sound by dragging its right edge

Stretching and Repeating

If you stretch a short sound (like a kick) across multiple beats, it will repeat to fill the space. For example, a kick stretched to 4 beats will play 4 times, once per beat. This is great for quickly laying down a steady pattern.

Color Coding

Each sound is color-coded by its category. Kicks are red, bass is green, synths are blue, and so on. This makes it easy to visually scan your arrangement and see what's where.

One Sound Per Cell

Each cell (1 beat x 1 row) can hold one sound. If you want multiple sounds playing at the same time, place them on different rows at the same beat position. That's what the rows are for: layering.

5. Editing Sounds

Opening the Editor

Double-click any placed sound on the grid to open the sound editor panel at the bottom of the screen.

Available Controls

  • Pitch - Shift the pitch up or down by semitones (-24 to +24)
  • Fine Tune - Micro-adjust pitch in cents (-50 to +50)
  • Volume - Adjust loudness (0% to 150%)
  • Pan - Position in the stereo field (L100 to R100)
  • Attack - Fade-in time (0 to 500ms)
  • Decay - Fade-out time (0 to 2000ms)
  • Filter Type - None, Low Pass, High Pass, Band Pass
  • Cutoff - Filter frequency (20Hz to 20kHz)
  • Resonance - Filter emphasis (0 to 20)
  • Distortion - Add grit and drive (0% to 100%)
  • Reverse - Play the sound backwards
  • Stretch - Speed up or slow down (0.25x to 4x)

Apply Scope

When you change a setting, you can choose how widely it applies:

  • This beat - Only the sound you double-clicked (default)
  • All of this sound - Every instance of this sound on the grid
  • This bar - All sounds in the same 4-beat bar
  • Selected beats - All sounds in your current multi-selection

Preview

Click the play button in the editor header to hear the sound with your current modifications before closing the editor.

Save as WAV

Click "Save as WAV" in the editor to download the modified sound as a standalone audio file.

6. Selection and Copy/Paste

Selecting Sounds

  • Single click - Select one sound (highlights it with a white border)
  • Click empty space - Deselect all

Multi-Select

Click and drag on empty space to draw a selection rectangle (like selecting cells in a spreadsheet). All sounds within the rectangle will be selected. This works across multiple rows and beats.

Copy, Cut, Paste

  • Ctrl+C - Copy the selected sounds
  • Ctrl+X - Cut (copy and delete the originals)
  • Click a target cell, then Ctrl+V - Paste at that position

Pasted sounds maintain their relative positions. If you selected sounds across rows 3-7 and beats 4-12, they'll paste with the same spacing.

Duplicate with Ctrl+Click

Select one or more sounds, then Ctrl+Click on a selected sound to duplicate the entire selection. Copies are placed to the right of the originals.

Moving Sounds

Click a sound to select it, then drag it to move it to a different cell. You can move sounds horizontally (different beat) or vertically (different row).

Deleting Sounds

Select one or more sounds, then press Delete or Backspace to remove them. You can also right-click and choose "Delete" from the context menu.

7. Playback and Transport

Play/Pause

Click the play button or press Space. The playhead (red line) moves across the grid, playing all sounds it passes through. Click again to pause.

Stop

Click the stop button to stop playback and reset the playhead to the beginning.

Loop

Click the loop button to toggle looping. When active, playback will loop from the start to the last placed sound (or your custom loop region).

BPM

Adjust the tempo using the BPM field. Common tempos: House 120-130, Dubstep 140-150, Drum and Bass 170-180.

Snap

The snap setting controls how sounds align to the grid when placing, moving, or resizing:

  • 1 Beat - Snap to whole beats
  • 1/2 Beat - Snap to half beats
  • 1/4 Beat - Snap to quarter beats (default, good for detailed work)
  • 1/8 Beat - Snap to eighth beats (very fine)
  • Off - Free placement (no snapping)

Mute and Solo

Each row has M (mute) and S (solo) buttons:

  • Mute - Silences that row during playback. Useful for testing how a track sounds without certain elements.
  • Solo - Only play this row (and other soloed rows). Everything else is muted. Great for isolating a bass line or drum pattern.

8. Zoom and Navigation

Horizontal Zoom (Beat Width)

Hold Ctrl and scroll the mouse wheel to zoom in/out on the timeline. This makes beats wider or narrower, letting you see more or less of your arrangement.

Vertical Zoom (Row Height)

Hold Ctrl+Shift and scroll the mouse wheel to change row height. Useful when you have many rows and want to see more at once.

Scrolling

Scroll normally to move through the grid. The ruler at the top stays in sync with horizontal scrolling. Row labels stick to the left as you scroll right.

9. Templates

Click the "Templates" button to browse 40 pre-built track arrangements across four genres:

  • Drum and Bass (10 templates) - Fast breaks, rolling bass, atmospheric builds
  • House (10 templates) - 4-on-the-floor grooves, deep bass, warm pads
  • Dubstep (10 templates) - Half-time beats, heavy bass drops, dramatic builds
  • Electronic (10 templates) - Creative patterns, synth-driven, atmospheric

Each template is a full arrangement (intro, build, drop, breakdown, second drop, outro). Loading a template replaces your current work, so save first if needed.

Tip: Templates are a great starting point. Load one, then modify it by swapping sounds, changing patterns, adjusting the editor settings, and making it your own.

10. Saving and Versions

Manual Save

Click "Save" (or Ctrl+S) to download your project as a JSON file. This saves all your sounds, placements, and editor settings.

Loading a Project

Click "Load" to open a previously saved project file.

Autosave

Your work is automatically saved every 10 minutes. The system keeps the last 9 versions (covering the past 90 minutes of work).

Version History

Click "Versions" to see your autosaved versions. Each shows the timestamp and number of blocks. Click one to restore it.

Tip: If you've been experimenting for 30 minutes and don't like the results, open Versions and restore the state from 30 minutes ago. Your experiments are gone but your earlier good work is preserved.

Undo/Redo

Press Ctrl+Z to undo the last action. Press Ctrl+Shift+Z to redo. You can undo as many times as you want, all the way back to when you started.

11. Exporting

Export WAV

Click "Export WAV" to render your entire arrangement as a WAV audio file. This processes all sounds with their editor settings, respects mute/solo, and outputs a stereo 44.1kHz 16-bit WAV file.

The export covers from beat 1 to the last placed sound. Muted rows are excluded, soloed rows are respected.

Save Individual Sounds

Open the sound editor (double-click a sound), tweak it, then click "Save as WAV" to export just that one modified sound.

13. Production Tips

Building a Track

  1. Start with the beat. Lay down a kick pattern on row 1, snare on row 2, hats on row 3. Get 4-8 bars of a basic groove.
  2. Add bass. Drop a bass sound on row 6-7, matching the rhythm of your kick or creating a complementary pattern.
  3. Layer up. Add synths, pads, or piano on rows 10+. Use the sound editor to adjust pitch and filter to make things sit together.
  4. Copy and paste. Select your 8-bar section, copy it, and paste it further along the timeline. Now you have 16 bars.
  5. Create variation. In the second 8 bars, add or remove elements. Maybe add a clap, change the hi-hat pattern, or bring in a new synth.
  6. Build the arrangement. Use the intro-build-drop-breakdown-drop-outro structure. Start sparse, add energy, peak, pull back, peak again, wind down.

Genre-Specific Tips

  • House (120-130 BPM): Kick on every beat (4-on-the-floor). Clap/snare on beats 2 and 4. Offbeat hi-hats. Deep, rolling bass. Warm pads.
  • Drum and Bass (170-180 BPM): Fast breakbeats. Kick and snare alternate rapidly. Rolling or neuro basslines. Atmospheric pads and FX. Use the DnB kick and snare sounds.
  • Dubstep (140-150 BPM): Half-time feel (kick on 1, snare on 3). Heavy wobble or growl bass in the drop. Build tension with risers before the drop. Sparse intro, massive drops.
  • Electronic (125-140 BPM): Flexible. Experiment with unconventional patterns. Layer synths. Use FX liberally. Automate filters for movement.

Sound Design Tips

  • Pitch shifting a kick down 3-5 semitones makes it deeper and heavier
  • Low-pass filter on a hi-hat at around 8kHz removes harshness
  • Distortion on a bass at 20-40% adds warmth and presence
  • Reverse a cymbal crash and place it before a drop for a classic build effect
  • Stretch a pad to 0.5x to make it slower and more atmospheric
  • Layer sounds on adjacent rows at the same beat position for thicker textures
Watch your levels: If you have many sounds playing at once, the mix can clip. Use the volume control in the editor to bring down individual sounds, especially bass and kick which carry the most energy.
